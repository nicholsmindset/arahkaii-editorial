# Editorial Pillars — Arahkaii (8-pillar architecture)

Replaces the previous 6-pillar structure (Fashion / Beauty / Culture / Travel / Wellness / Sustainability). The 8-pillar version aligns to the validated Ahrefs clusters and the Muslim-owned brand positioning.

**Wellness** folds into Beauty as a sub-cluster.
**Sustainability** becomes a cross-cutting theme (not a standalone pillar).
**Dining** is new — and is the highest-ROI cluster in the entire site (CNA Luxury's Mother's Day Dining article alone pulls 8,537 visits/month; the halal version of this is Arahkaii's biggest single Q2 opportunity).
**Living, People, Guides** are new pillars carved out from what was previously buried inside Culture or Fashion.

## Pillar 1 — Style

**Replaces:** Fashion (broader, more modest-inclusive).

**Coverage:** Asian designers, modest luxury labels, runway analysis (PFW, MFW, LFW, NYFW + Seoul, Shanghai, Tokyo, Jakarta, KL Modest Fashion Week), luxury houses' Asia plays, investment-grade pieces, hijab styling, modest swimwear, watches & jewellery as adornment.

**Voice register:** Insider analytical, knows industry plumbing, opinionated about cut and drape. See VOICE.md §4.1.

**Recurring formats:**
- Designer profiles ("Inside [House]'s Quiet Revolution")
- Modest-luxury movement pieces
- Fashion week analysis (post-show, not live-blog)
- Investment frames ("The Modest Resortwear Brands Defining 2026")
- Hijab and modest styling explainers

**Out of scope:** Pure shopping listicles · celebrity outfit recaps · trend pieces without a thesis · revealing-fashion editorials.

**Adjacent reading:** Vogue, Vogue Arabia, Vogue Business, Business of Fashion, *Tatler Asia* fashion section.

## Pillar 2 — Beauty

**Coverage:** K-beauty, J-beauty, C-beauty as three propositions. Skincare science. Heritage practices (TCM, kampo, Korean court traditions, Middle Eastern apothecary). Halal-certified brands. PDRN, exosomes, salmon DNA, new launches with editorial eye.

**Includes (formerly its own pillar):** Wellness — Asian wellness traditions, longevity, fasting (Ramadan-relevant), hammam, hot-spring culture, sober wellness practices.

**Voice register:** Educated skeptic, clinically literate, halal-aware. See VOICE.md §4.2.

**Recurring formats:**
- Ingredient deep-dives (PDRN, centella, propolis, niacinamide)
- Treatment explainers (salmon DNA facial, exosomes, lasers, microneedling)
- Brand profiles — *with halal-certification status stated plainly*
- Where-to-get-it guides (SG / KL / Jakarta / Dubai)
- Wellness practice explainers (fasting frameworks, hammam ritual, hot-spring culture)

**Out of scope:** Single-product reviews without context · "holy grail" listicles · influencer-quoting trend pieces · Goop-flavoured wellness.

**Adjacent reading:** Allure, Glossy, Into The Gloss (vintage), Sulwhasoo's own editorial site.

## Pillar 3 — Dining ★ (new, highest ROI)

**Coverage:** Halal fine dining, Muslim-owned restaurants, halal omakase & tasting menus, dessert bars, specialty coffee bars, chefs, new openings, food guides across SG / KL / JB / Bali / Dubai / Tokyo. Tea-pairing menus. Cross-border (Singapore → JB) food editorial.

**This pillar replaces all alcohol/bar coverage.** It is where the Halal Substitution Framework lives in practice.

**Voice register:** Quiet restaurant critic — has eaten the menu twice. See VOICE.md §4.3.

**Recurring formats:**
- Best halal fine dining lists (SG, KL, JB, Jakarta)
- Mother's Day / Eid / Ramadan dining round-ups
- Muslim-owned restaurant profiles
- Halal omakase deep-dives
- New restaurant openings — *with halal status stated plainly*
- Tea-pairing menus, dessert-pairing menus
- Specialty coffee bar guides
- Hidden dessert bar lists
- Food guides for specific neighbourhoods

**Mandatory in every Dining article:** explicit halal status of each restaurant — halal-certified · Muslim-owned · alcohol-free · pork-free with alcohol in cooking · non-halal (with reason for inclusion).

**Out of scope:** Wine pairing · cocktail bars · beer guides · whisky tastings · brewery tours · "best bars" lists in any form.

**Adjacent reading:** The Infatuation Asia, *CNA Luxury* Experiences section (dining only), *Tatler* dining sections, Eater Asia.

## Pillar 4 — Travel

**Coverage:** Asia-Pacific luxury + modest-friendly travel, decompression / slow hotels, hotel-room-level reportage, restaurant criticism in destinations, shopping geography. Always with a modest-traveller lens: where to pray, where to find halal food, what modest dress works locally.

**Voice register:** Slow correspondent who has actually stayed three nights. See VOICE.md §4.4.

**Recurring formats:**
- City guides (Tokyo, Kyoto, Seoul, Bangkok, Bali, Singapore, KL, Jakarta, Hong Kong, Istanbul, Doha, Dubai)
- "Where to eat, shop, stay and play in [city]" CNA-Luxury-template guides
- New-luxury-property reviews
- Weekend escape format (JB, Penang, Bandung, Pulau Tioman)
- The Modest Traveller series — explicitly Muslim-friendly Bali, Bangkok, Tokyo, Istanbul
- Cross-border travel (SG → JB, KL → Penang)

**Mandatory in every Travel piece:** a "For the Modest Traveller" sidebar covering halal food, prayer facilities, modest-dress climate notes, alcohol-free venue alternatives.

**Out of scope:** "Hidden gem" listicles · bucket-list filler · influencer itineraries · nightlife/bar guides in any form.

**Adjacent reading:** Condé Nast Traveler (selectively), Wallpaper* travel, *CNA Luxury* Experiences, *Mr Hudson*, *Halal Travel Media*.

## Pillar 5 — Living

**Coverage:** Quiet-luxury homes, interiors, art collecting, design movements (Bottega Veneta x Cassina, The Row's apartments, Lemaire's home line). Specific Singapore / KL / Jakarta apartment tours. Materials, plans, light.

**Voice register:** Design diary — friend of the architect on a slow Saturday. See VOICE.md §4.5.

**Recurring formats:**
- Quiet luxury home tours (named architect, named neighbourhood, specific materials)
- Interior pieces ("The Singapore Apartments Designed for Quiet Luxury")
- Design hotel interiors (overlap with Travel — primary tag goes to whichever angle leads)
- Art and object collecting

**Out of scope:** "Dream home" listicles · open-house tours of generic developer flats · maximalist "Insta-worthy" interior content.

**Adjacent reading:** *AD Middle East*, *Wallpaper*, *Tatler Homes*, NY Times T Magazine design pieces.

## Pillar 6 — People

**Coverage:** Founders, designers, artists, tastemakers, especially Muslim entrepreneurs in premium F&B / fashion / beauty / hospitality. Day-in-the-life formats. Studio visits. Profile interviews.

**Voice register:** Patient profile voice — sat with the subject for two hours. See VOICE.md §4.6.

**Recurring formats:**
- Founder profile ("How [name] built [brand]")
- Designer studio visits
- Tastemaker series ("The Stylish Asian Women Redefining [X]")
- Day-in-the-life (Tatler / CNA-proven format)
- The Modest Leaders series — explicitly Muslim-owned premium brands

**Out of scope:** Celebrity gossip · dating rumours · paparazzi-style coverage · puff pieces without specific reportage.

**Adjacent reading:** *Tatler* people sections, *CNA Luxury* People, *Vogue Arabia* profiles, *Monocle*'s founder profiles.

## Pillar 7 — Culture

**Coverage:** Commentary, cultural movements, modest-fashion economics, Asian identity, the meta-conversation about luxury, K-drama as cultural signal (not gossip), Asian craft heritage.

**Voice register:** Cultural columnist — argument-led, evidence-rich. See VOICE.md §4.7.

**Recurring formats:**
- Cultural movement essays ("The Quiet Renaissance of [X]")
- The Modest Movement series (the rise of modest luxury as a cultural force)
- Asian craft heritage deep-dives
- Trend-as-signal pieces (with three named examples per claim)
- Anniversary / retrospective pieces

**Out of scope:** Listicles of "Asian-inspired" trends · anything that flattens a culture · tourist-voice cultural observations · K-pop / K-drama gossip.

**Adjacent reading:** *AnOther Magazine* culture, *T Magazine*, *Tatler Asia* culture, *Highsnobiety*'s long-reads.

## Pillar 8 — Guides

**Coverage:** Service journalism — best-of lists, where-to-go, what-to-buy, seasonal edits, the signature **Arahkaii Evening Edit** (the alcohol-free nightlife play).

**Voice register:** Practical service-journalism — patient editor who has done the homework. See VOICE.md §4.8.

**Recurring formats:**
- "Best of [X] in [city] 2026" listicles
- Seasonal edits (Eid gift guide, Ramadan dining guide, Mother's Day, Mid-Autumn)
- The Arahkaii Evening Edit (things-to-do-at-night without bars)
- Shopping geography
- Buyer's guides (modest swimwear, hijab fabrics, halal skincare)

**Mandatory in every Guide:** every entry lists halal / modest status. See VOICE.md §4.8.

**Out of scope:** Generic "ultimate guide" listicles · clickbait-ranked content · listicles where the writer hasn't actually verified the entries.

**Adjacent reading:** *CNA Lifestyle* guides, *Tatler* best-of lists, *The Infatuation* lists.

## Sustainability as cross-cutting theme

Sustainability is no longer its own pillar. It runs through:

- **Style** — modest-luxury investment dressing, vintage, second-hand luxury
- **Beauty** — refillable packaging, clean formulations, halal + clean dual-certification
- **Dining** — local sourcing, zero-waste kitchens, halal sustainable seafood
- **Travel** — slow hotels, train travel, modest-traveller eco stays
- **Living** — material provenance, heirloom design
- **People** — founder profiles that lead with sustainability practice
- **Culture** — the modest-luxury-as-anti-fast-fashion argument
- **Guides** — "best sustainable [X]" lists

**Use the tag `#sustainable` rather than a pillar.**
