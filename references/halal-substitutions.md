# Halal Substitution Framework

> Loaded by every skill that produces content. This is the rulebook for "what we cover instead" when a topic naturally pulls toward alcohol or nightlife.

## The rule in one line

**Arahkaii never covers alcohol or nightclub culture.** When a topic legitimately pulls there (a destination's "after dark" scene, "where tastemakers drink", "best bars", "wine pairings", "champagne lists"), we redirect to a halal-aligned cluster that chases the **same reader intent** without violating brand values.

## When the topic *cannot* be substituted

If a request legitimately requires alcohol coverage (e.g. partner brief, a paid placement, a story whose central subject is a winery), **refuse and explain**:

> "Arahkaii does not cover alcohol, bars, or nightclub content as part of our editorial position. We can offer the following alternatives that target the same reader: [list 2–3 from the table above]."

Never produce the alcoholic version and apologise for it. Never produce a "lighter" version that still names drinks. Never feature alcohol bottles in imagery.

---

## When alcohol appears tangentially

Some legitimate stories mention alcohol in passing — a Michelin restaurant that happens to have a wine list, a hotel with a bar on the 50th floor. The rule:

1. **Cover the restaurant / hotel, not the drink.** Lead with the kitchen, the chef, the room, the view.
2. **State the halal position once, plainly.** Example: *"The restaurant is not halal-certified and serves alcohol — skip the bar and ask for the kitchen counter."*
3. **Never describe drinks.** No tasting notes. No bartender profiles. No "the cocktail menu is excellent".
4. **Imagery never includes alcohol.** Pick the kitchen, the dish, the room — not the bar.

## Imagery enforcement

Reject any image (AI-generated or stock) that contains:

- Wine glasses, beer glasses, cocktail glasses, champagne flutes
- Bottles of any alcoholic drink (including decoratively in a kitchen scene)
- Bar interiors centred on liquor shelves
- Nightclub lighting, dance floors, crowds at clubs
- "Raised glass" gestures of any kind, even with water
- Any scene that reads as "after-party"

Replace with:
- Tea ware (kyusu, gaiwan, glass teapot)
- Espresso cups, latte glasses with art
- Dates, pomegranates, figs in still life
- Mocktail glassware (clear, no garnish that mimics cocktails)
- Candlelight (single candle, not bar mood lighting)
- Hotel lobby lounges, dining-led rooms
- Pastry, chocolate, ceramic plating
- Linen napkins, brushed brass, ceramic ware
