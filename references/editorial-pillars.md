# Editorial Pillars — arahkaii.com

The six coverage areas, with what each pillar covers, why it exists, and how the voice shifts between them. Read alongside `brand-voice.md`.

---

## Pillar 1 — Fashion

**Coverage:** Asian designers and craft, runway analysis (PFW, MFW, LFW, NYFW + Seoul/Shanghai/Tokyo where relevant), luxury houses' Asia plays, investment-grade pieces, trend-as-cultural-signal analysis.

**Voice register:** Insider analytical. Knows the industry's plumbing. Argues, doesn't describe.

**Frequency target:** 40% of content output.

**Recurring formats:**
- Designer profiles ("Inside [House]'s Quiet Revolution")
- Trend reportage ("The Quiet Renaissance of Korean Heritage Brands")
- Fashion week analysis (post-show, not live-blog)
- Investment frames ("Future Vintage: The Five Bags That Will Define 2030")
- Industry analysis ("Why Daniel Lee Leaving Burberry Is the End of an Era")

**What's out of scope:**
- Pure shopping listicles ("10 Best Bags Under $500")
- Celebrity outfit recaps without analysis
- Trend pieces without a thesis

**Adjacent reading:** Vogue, Vogue Business, Business of Fashion, Highsnowsiety, The Cut

---

## Pillar 2 — Beauty

**Coverage:** K-beauty, J-beauty, C-beauty as three distinct propositions. Skincare science. Heritage practices (TCM, kampo, Korean court traditions). New launches with editorial eye, not press-release voice.

**Voice register:** Educated skeptic. Knows niacinamide from tranexamic acid. Knows propolis. Knows snail mucin's actual mechanism. Reads dermatology papers.

**Frequency target:** 20% of content output.

**Recurring formats:**
- "The X-beauty Second Wave" (movement explainers)
- Hero ingredient deep-dives ("Why Centella Is K-Beauty's Most Misunderstood Hero")
- Brand profiles (Sulwhasoo, Hada Labo, Florasis)
- Where-to-buy guides (Singapore-, Jakarta-, KL-specific)
- Trend explainers (Skinflu, glass skin's evolution, the slugging debate)

**What's out of scope:**
- Single-product reviews without context
- "Holy grail" listicles
- Influencer-quoting trend pieces
- Marketing-claim restatement

**Adjacent reading:** Glossy, Allure, Into The Gloss (vintage), Sulwhasoo's own editorial site

---

## Pillar 3 — Culture

**Coverage:** Heritage craft, Asian luxury's intellectual history, designer-as-cultural-figure profiles, the meta-conversation about what luxury means in Asia.

**Voice register:** Cultural critic. Historically literate. Reads as someone with actual ideas about heritage, modernity, and the politics of taste.

**Frequency target:** 15% of content output.

**Recurring formats:**
- Atelier walkthroughs (synthesized reportage from real workshops where possible)
- Designer cultural profiles ("How Loewe Became the Most Asian-Sensibility European House")
- Movement essays ("The Modest Fashion Economy")
- Heritage explainers (the difference between Peranakan, Baba-Nyonya, and Straits Chinese; what makes a kimono not a robe)

**What's out of scope:**
- Listicles of "Asian-inspired" trends
- Anything that flattens a culture to an aesthetic
- Tourist-voice cultural observations

**Adjacent reading:** AnOther Magazine's culture sections, NY Times T Magazine, Tatler Asia's culture pieces

---

## Pillar 4 — Travel

**Coverage:** Asia-Pacific luxury, the new-luxury-stay format (decompression stays, slow hotels), specific hotel-room-level reportage, restaurant criticism in fashion-adjacent destinations, shopping geography.

**Voice register:** Repeat-visitor editor. Stayed there. Knows the room to request.

**Frequency target:** 10% of content output.

**Recurring formats:**
- "The Real X" insider guides (Tokyo, Kyoto, Seoul, Bangkok, Bali, Singapore, KL, Jakarta, Hong Kong, Hanoi)
- New-luxury-property reviews
- Trend reportage ("The Slow Hotel Boom")
- Shopping geography (where Bottega Veneta's Tokyo concept is, the new Loewe house in Seoul)

**What's out of scope:**
- Generic "best hotels in [city]" listicles
- Bucket-list travel listicles
- Influencer-itinerary content
- Anything that uses "hidden gem"

**Adjacent reading:** Condé Nast Traveler (selectively), The Aficionado, Mr Hudson, Wallpaper*'s travel section

---

## Pillar 5 — Lifestyle / Wellness

**Coverage:** Fasting (Robert's personal expertise), longevity, slow living, wellness as practice not trend. Considered. Counter-cultural to influencer wellness.

**Voice register:** Pragmatic. Skeptical of wellness-industrial complex while taking the underlying practices seriously.

**Frequency target:** 10% of content output.

**Recurring formats:**
- Practice explainers (intermittent fasting frameworks, mindful eating in Asian context)
- Counter-trend pieces ("Why the Cold Plunge Trend Is Mostly Bullshit", but in Tatler register)
- Profile pieces (Asia's longevity researchers, fasting practitioners)
- Heritage wellness (TCM, ayurveda, hammam traditions, hot-spring culture)

**What's out of scope:**
- Anything Goop-flavored
- Cleanse/detox content
- "Self-care" listicles
- Influencer-quoting wellness pieces

**Adjacent reading:** Cured Magazine, Aēsop Journal, The Cut's health pieces (selectively)

---

## Pillar 6 — Sustainability / Design

**Coverage:** Sustainable luxury (not greenwashing), heritage-craft preservation, quiet-design movements (Bottega Veneta, The Row, Lemaire), the case for buying less and better.

**Voice register:** Pragmatic, not preachy. Frames sustainability as quality and longevity.

**Frequency target:** 5% of content output.

**Recurring formats:**
- Brand sustainability profiles (Loewe's craft program, Hermès' atelier system, Stella McCartney without rose-tinting)
- Material explainers (the actual lifecycle of vicuña, what makes Sea Island cotton special, why intrecciato is durable)
- Investment-as-sustainability arguments

**What's out of scope:**
- Generic "10 sustainable brands" listicles
- Greenwashing brand profiles
- Preachy moralizing
- Generation-bashing content

**Adjacent reading:** The Sustainable Fashion Collective, Vogue Business sustainability section

## Cross-pillar conventions

**Coverage that touches multiple pillars** (e.g., a piece on Loewe's Galician craft program touches Fashion + Culture + Sustainability) — assign **one primary pillar** for taxonomy/category, allow tags from other pillars.

## Pillar balance check

Routine 3 (Weekly Performance Review) checks pillar distribution monthly. Target ratios:

- Fashion: 40%
- Beauty: 20%
- Culture: 15%
- Travel: 10%
- Lifestyle: 10%
- Sustainability: 5%

Deviation >10% in either direction in any 30-day window triggers a calendar review.
