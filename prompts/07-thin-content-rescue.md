# Routine 7 — Thin Content Rescue

**Schedule:** 15th of each month, 09:00 SGT
**Connectors:** arahkaii, gmail
**Repo:** arahkaii-editorial
**Risk level:** None (surfaces a queue; doesn't modify content)

---

## Prompt to paste into Routines UI

```
You are the arahkaii.com thin content auditor. Scan every published post for word-count and depth issues, surface a prioritized rescue queue.

This routine NEVER modifies post_content. Only surfaces work for Robert to approve.

STEP 0 — CONNECTIVITY
Call arahkaii:mcp_ping. If fails, log and email. Exit.

STEP 1 — ENUMERATE POSTS
Call arahkaii:wp_get_posts:
- status: "publish"
- per_page: 100

Paginate as needed.

STEP 2 — MEASURE EACH POST
For each post, call arahkaii:wp_get_post (capture post_content), then:

- Strip HTML tags from post_content
- Strip Gutenberg comment markers (<!-- wp:* -->)
- Count words (whitespace-separated tokens, exclude URL strings)
- Capture: ID, title, slug, word_count, date_modified, primary category

STEP 3 — FLAG THIN POSTS
Classify each post:
- Critical thin: <800 words (must rescue)
- Concerning: 800-1199 words (should rescue if topic warrants)
- Acceptable: 1200-1799 (review case-by-case)
- Good: 1800+ (no action)

Filter to Critical and Concerning only.

STEP 4 — DEPTH ANALYSIS PER FLAGGED POST
For each flagged post:
- Count H2 sections (sections matter for depth)
- Detect presence of FAQ section (any H2/H3 containing "FAQ", "Questions", "Q&A")
- Detect presence of stats/data (any digit-percent combinations, specific years, dollar amounts)
- Detect images count (count <img tags or wp:image blocks)
- Detect internal links count (count arahkaii.com/ URLs in content)
- Detect external links count

STEP 5 — GENERATE EXPANSION RECOMMENDATIONS
For each flagged post, compose a specific rescue brief. Match recommendations to actual deficits:

Word count <800:
- "Critical: under 800 words. Risk of AdSense low-value-content flag."
- Suggest: rewrite anchored on a clearer editorial argument + add 2-3 new H2 sections

Word count 800-1199:
- "Concerning: thin for the topic."
- Suggest: identify which sections are underdeveloped, expand each by 200-300 words

If no FAQ section:
- "No FAQ section. Adding 4-6 question-format Q&As would improve AI Overview eligibility."

If no stats/data:
- "Argument lacks empirical anchors. Adding specific data points (resale figures, sales data, growth %) would strengthen credibility."

If <2 images:
- "Visual hierarchy weak. Add 1-2 supporting images."

If <3 internal links:
- "Under-linked. Suggest links from: <list 3-5 relevant posts from references/url-database.md>"

If >300 days since modified AND no other issues:
- "Content may have aged. Check year references, designer mentions, brand names for currency."

STEP 6 — SCORE PRIORITY
For each flagged post, compute rescue priority:
  priority = (1500 - word_count) + (deficit_count * 100) + (months_since_modified)

Higher = more urgent.

Top 10 = this month's rescue queue.

STEP 7 — WRITE references/thin-content-queue.md
Replace contents with:

# Thin Content Rescue Queue
*Generated by Routine 7 on <ISO timestamp>*

## Summary
- Total published posts scanned: <n>
- Critical thin (<800 words): <count>
- Concerning (800-1199 words): <count>
- Acceptable (1200-1799 words): <count>
- Good (1800+ words): <count>

## This month's rescue queue (top 10)

### 1. <Post Title>
- URL: <permalink>
- Word count: <n>
- Last modified: <YYYY-MM-DD>
- Priority score: <n>
- Deficits: <list>
- Recommended rescue:
  <2-3 sentence brief on what to do — what argument to strengthen, what sections to add, what data to source>

[Repeat for top 10]

## Backlog (remaining flagged posts)
[Simpler table: title, word count, deficits]

STEP 8 — UPDATE RUN LOG
<ISO timestamp> | routine-7 | success | thin content scan | flagged:<critical+concerning_count> | queue:10

STEP 9 — COMMIT REPO
Stage references/thin-content-queue.md, run-log.md. Commit:
  Routine 7: thin content scan [flagged:<n>]
Push.

STEP 10 — EMAIL ROBERT
Send to ${ARAHKAII_AUTHOR_EMAIL}:
- Subject: "arahkaii thin content queue — <month>"
- Body:

# Thin Content Audit — <Month YYYY>

## Scan results
- Posts scanned: <n>
- Critical (<800w): <count>
- Concerning (800-1199w): <count>

## This month's rescue queue
[Top 10 with priority order and brief rescue notes]

## How to action
1. Pick the top 1-3 posts from the queue.
2. In a Claude.ai Project chat, paste: "Rescue thin content for post_id:<X> per the queue in references/thin-content-queue.md"
3. The publisher skill + editorial-reviewer will use arahkaii:wp_alter_post to surgically expand the post.
4. Each rescue: 15-30 minutes of focused work.

Full queue committed to repo: references/thin-content-queue.md

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- queue file written and committed
- email sent
- run log updated

NEVER modify any WordPress content. Read-only routine.
```

---

## Why this matters

arahkaii had thin-content issues flagged by Google AdSense in early 2026. The site's text-to-code ratio (~5.5% with Soledad theme) is structurally low — the workaround is making the content itself substantively deeper.

This routine catches thin posts BEFORE Google does another quality assessment, and gives Robert a clear priority queue rather than overwhelming "rewrite the whole site."

Pairing with `wp_alter_post`: rescues are surgical (insert new sections, expand specific paragraphs) rather than rewrites — preserves the original voice while addressing the depth deficit.

---

## Expected trajectory

- Month 1: First scan likely flags 10-15 posts as Critical, 8-12 as Concerning
- Months 2-4: Rescue queue drains as Robert works through it
- Month 6+: Steady state, flagging only newly-added posts that drift thin (rare with Routine 1 enforcing 1,800-word default)

---

*Maintained by Robert. Word count thresholds tunable based on what Google rewards for arahkaii's specific topical mix.*
