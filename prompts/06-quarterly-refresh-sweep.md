# Routine 6 — Quarterly Refresh Sweep + URL Database Rebuild

**Schedule:** 1st of January, April, July, October — 09:00 SGT
**Connectors:** arahkaii, ahrefs, gmail
**Repo:** arahkaii-editorial
**Risk level:** None on site (read-only). Writes only to repo files.

PART B — REFRESH CANDIDATES

STEP 5 — IDENTIFY DECAY
Filter posts where post_modified is >180 days ago.

For each, call ahrefs:site-explorer-organic-keywords for the URL with date_compared = 90 days ago. Capture:
- Current monthly traffic estimate
- Traffic 90 days ago
- Traffic delta (absolute and %)
- Top ranking keyword and its position trend

STEP 6 — SCORE REFRESH CANDIDATES
Compute refresh score for each post:
  score = (days_since_modified / 30) + (traffic_drop_% / 10) + (10 if top keyword position > 10 else 0)

Higher score = higher refresh priority.

Top 15 = quarterly refresh queue.

## Why this routine matters

The URL database is the single most-referenced file by Routine 1 (drafting). When it's accurate, internal linking is precise. When it's stale, internal links go to outdated posts or miss recent additions.

The refresh queue addresses content decay — the slow, invisible loss of traffic on posts that were once strong. Without quarterly attention, decay compounds into significant traffic loss over a year.
